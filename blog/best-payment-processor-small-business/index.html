How to Choose the Right Payment Processor

Here are the key factors to consider:

1. Your Business Type

A restaurant has different needs than a retail store or online business. Choose a processor that specializes in your industry.

2. Processing Volume

Higher volume = more leverage to negotiate rates. If you process over $10,000/month, you should be getting better than flat-rate pricing.

3. Total Cost of Ownership

Don't just look at the processing rate. Consider monthly fees, equipment costs, PCI compliance fees, chargeback fees, and any other hidden costs.

4. Contract Terms

Avoid long-term contracts with early termination fees. Your processor should earn your business every month.

5. Added Value

Some processors offer more than just payment processing. Marketing services, websites, inventory management â€” these add-ons can provide significant value.

6. Support Quality

When something goes wrong on a busy Saturday night, you need help fast. 24/7 human support is essential.
