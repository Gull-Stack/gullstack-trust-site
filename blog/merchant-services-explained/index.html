How Merchant Services Work

When a customer pays with a card at your business, here's what happens behind the scenes:

1. Customer Initiates Payment

The customer swipes, inserts, taps, or manually enters their card information at your POS terminal or online checkout.

2. Data Transmission

Your payment gateway encrypts the card data and sends it to the payment processor, which routes it through the card network (Visa, Mastercard, etc.) to the customer's bank.

3. Authorization

The customer's bank verifies the card is valid, checks for available funds, and sends back an approval or decline within seconds.

4. Settlement

At the end of the day, all approved transactions are "batched" and sent for settlement. Funds are transferred from the customer's bank to your merchant account, then to your business bank account.

Types of Merchant Accounts

Traditional Merchant Account

A dedicated account from a bank or independent processor. Requires application and underwriting. Best for established businesses with higher volume.

Payment Service Provider (PSP)

Companies like Square, Stripe, and PayPal that let you process payments through their master merchant account. Easier setup, but typically higher fees and less control.

High-Risk Merchant Account

Special accounts for businesses in industries considered high-risk (CBD, travel, subscription services). Higher fees but necessary for certain business types.

Choosing the Right Merchant Services Provider

Not all merchant services are created equal. Here's what to evaluate:

Pricing Structure

Understand whether they use flat-rate, interchange-plus, or tiered pricing. For most businesses, interchange-plus offers the best value and transparency.

Contract Terms

Watch out for long-term contracts and early termination fees. The best providers offer month-to-month agreements.

Equipment Costs

Some providers give "free" equipment but lock you into expensive leases. Buy your equipment outright when possible.

Integration

Make sure the provider integrates with your existing POS system, accounting software, and e-commerce platform.

Support Quality

When your terminal goes down, you need help fast. Look for 24/7 support with real humans, not just chatbots.
